"We made a mistake", a sentence that is shocking, unforgettable, and unacceptable,
makes me reflect on my life, and therefore make an impact on my way of thinking.

## The first time

### At the notary office 

I was at a local notary office, for notarizing high school credentials for my college application. 
There was a queue, in front of me was a high school student and his mother. 
The notary asked the student the purpose of the notarization, the student's mother answered for him, "for college application aboard".
The notary asked which country, the mother answered "the United Kingdom".
I'll be envy that the student can study in the UK, the second-best country in the world to study computer science, I thought.

It seemed smooth, soon after, it was my turn, I was alone, and I was asked the same question, I answered "for college application aboard", the notary asked which country,
I answered "India", since I heard Indian guys are doing great in Silicon Valley, and the new CEO of Microsoft is from India, same as Google's CEO.

The notary asked me a question, "Where did you buy your high school credentials?". I was shocked, "What? I received it from my high school, I didn't buy it." I answered.
"Your high school credentials are fake, you can't use it for college application." The notary said.
I know there are a lot of things are fake in China, but I didn't expect my high school credentials are fake because I received it from my high school, I thought.

I was trying to speculate why the notary said that. "Is it because I'm a borrower[^1]?", I was confused. "No, even a borrower's credentials should be valid." The notary answered.

### Effort I made

I've been prepared for this chance for a long time, India wasn't my first choice.
I was trying to study in the United States, but I couldn't find a way,
I asked an IELTS tutor, he said I should try to study in Australia.
I asked how much it costs, he said you should better prepare 500,000RMB.

Later I asked my parents for the money, they said they don't have that much money.
And my parents threaten me, "Do not get close to that tutor, he is a liar, he wants to cheat your money."
I'm motivated, self-driven and resilient, I didn't give up, I tried to find another way.
And I started trying my third option, India.

I've watched a lot of Bollywood movies.
"English Vinglish", "Three Idiots", "My Name Is Khan"...
I watch them all to understand India, as well as the Indian accent.

I was resisted about learning Indian accent in the beginning,
just like others would like to learn American accent and some others would like to learn British accent.
No one would like to learn Indian accent.
But after hearing a lot of Indian Software Engineers are in the Bay Area,
"What if they became my colleagues?", I thought.

And I have no necessary to learn how to speak it, I just need to understand it.
Even though if I place myself in an Indian environment, I can't avoid speaking it.
